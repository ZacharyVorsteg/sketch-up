## Part 1: Building Your Portfolio

### Why Portfolio Matters

**Your portfolio is your silent salesperson:**
- Shows capability
- Demonstrates style
- Proves professionalism
- Builds trust

**Without portfolio = "Who are you?"**  
**With portfolio = "When can you start?"**

---

### What to Include

**Minimum viable portfolio (3-5 projects):**

1. **Bedroom from Module 4**
   - Shows: Basic modeling, materials, space planning
   - 3-4 images

2. **Kitchen from Module 7**
   - Shows: Complex project, cabinetry, appliances
   - 4-6 images

3. **One self-directed project**
   - Living room, bathroom, patio, etc.
   - Shows initiative
   - 4-6 images

4. **Optional: Before/After**
   - Remodel scenario
   - Shows transformation value
   - 2 images per project

5. **Optional: Different style**
   - Traditional, modern, rustic, etc.
   - Shows versatility
   - 3-4 images

**Total: 15-25 high-quality images**

---

### Portfolio Image Quality Standards

**Technical requirements:**
- **Resolution:** 1920Ã—1080 minimum (export at 4000px)
- **Format:** JPG or PNG
- **File size:** 500KB - 2MB each (web-optimized)
- **Aspect ratio:** 16:9 or 4:3 (consistent)

**Composition rules:**
- Clean, uncluttered views
- Good lighting (shadows on)
- Interesting angles
- Shows scale (include furniture/people)
- Highlights design features

**What NOT to include:**
- Blurry images
- Bad lighting
- Incomplete models
- Learning exercises
- Amateur work

**Quality over quantity. Always.**

## Part 2: Pricing Your Services

### Understanding Your Value

**Common beginner mistake:** Underpricing

**Why it hurts:**
- Signals low quality
- Attracts problem clients
- Not sustainable
- Devalues industry

**Price based on value, not insecurity!**

---

### Pricing Models

**1. Hourly Rate**

**Formula:**
```
Desired Annual Income: $60,000
Ã· Billable Hours/Year: 1,000 hours (realistic)
= $60/hour minimum

Add overhead (30%) = $78/hour
Add profit margin (20%) = $94/hour

Round to: $100/hour
```

**Beginner rates:** $50-75/hour  
**Intermediate:** $75-125/hour  
**Expert:** $125-250/hour

**Pros:**
- Flexible for scope changes
- Fair for unknown projects
- Easy to calculate

**Cons:**
- Penalizes efficiency
- Clients watch the clock
- Hard to predict earnings

---

**2. Fixed Project Rate (Recommended)**

**Process:**
1. Estimate hours needed
2. Multiply by hourly rate
3. Add 20% buffer
4. Quote fixed price

**Example:**
```
Kitchen design estimate:
- Initial consultation: 1 hour
- Concept development: 4 hours
- 3D modeling: 8 hours
- Revisions: 4 hours
- Final presentation: 2 hours
Total: 19 hours Ã— $100 = $1,900
Buffer (20%): $380
Total quote: $2,280
Round to: $2,500
```

**Pros:**
- Client knows total cost
- You benefit from efficiency
- Professional
- Predictable income

**Cons:**
- Risk if scope grows
- Need accurate estimates
- Need clear scope definition

---

**3. Package Pricing (Best for Beginners)**

**Create service tiers:**

**Basic Package - $750**
- Single room design
- Up to 10' Ã— 12'
- Basic modeling
- 5 final images
- 1 round of revisions

**Standard Package - $1,500**
- Multi-room design OR complex single room
- Full materials specification
- Detailed modeling
- 10 final images
- 2 rounds of revisions

**Premium Package - $3,000+**
- Full home/large commercial
- Complete specifications
- Photorealistic renders
- Unlimited images
- 3 rounds of revisions
- Contractor documents

**Pros:**
- Clear options for clients
- Easy to explain
- Upsell opportunities
- Predictable workflow

**Cons:**
- May not fit all projects
- Need clear scope limits
- Customization requests

## Part 4: Client Communication

### The Initial Inquiry

**When someone contacts you:**

**1. Respond quickly** (within 24 hours, ideally 2 hours)
**2. Thank them**
**3. Ask qualifying questions:**
- What's the project?
- What's the timeline?
- What's the budget?
- Have you worked with a designer before?
- What's your style preference?

**4. Suggest next step:**
- Brief call (15-20 min, free)
- In-person consultation ($100-150 or free)
- Email questionnaire

---

### The Consultation

**Goals:**
1. Understand project fully
2. Build rapport
3. Showcase expertise
4. Determine if it's a good fit
5. Provide quote

**Bring:**
- Portfolio (digital or physical)
- Notepad
- Tape measure
- Camera (photos for reference)
- Contract template (if ready to sign)

**Ask:**
- What's working/not working now?
- What's the vision?
- Budget (specific number!)
- Timeline/deadline
- Decision makers (who approves?)
- Inspiration images

**Red flags:**
- No clear budget
- Unrealistic timeline
- Indecisive/unclear goals
- Previous designer problems (their fault)
- Disrespectful behavior

**Trust your gut. You can decline projects!**

---

### The Proposal

**Send within 48 hours of consultation:**

**Include:**
1. **Recap of project**
2. **Scope of work** (detailed!)
3. **Deliverables** (what they get)
4. **Timeline** (realistic!)
5. **Investment** (your price)
6. **Terms** (payment, revisions, etc.)
7. **Next steps** (how to proceed)

**Example scope:**
```
SCOPE OF WORK:

1. Space Planning
   - Measure existing space
   - Create 3D model of existing conditions
   - Develop 3 layout options
   - Client selects preferred option

2. Design Development
   - Refine selected layout
   - Select materials, finishes, fixtures
   - Create detailed 3D model
   - Present for client approval

3. Final Deliverables
   - 10 high-resolution rendered images
   - Floor plan with dimensions
   - Materials/finishes specification sheet
   - Optional: Shopping list with links

4. Revisions
   - 2 rounds of revisions included
   - Typical turnaround: 3-5 business days

TIMELINE: 3 weeks from contract signing

INVESTMENT: $1,500

PAYMENT TERMS: 50% deposit to start, 50% upon delivery
```

---

### Managing Revisions

**The revision killer:**
- Unlimited revisions = endless work
- Client can't decide
- You lose money

**The solution:**
1. **Include specific number** (2 rounds typical)
2. **Define what counts as revision:**
   - "Revision = changes to approved design"
   - "Major scope changes = new estimate"
3. **Charge for additional:**
   - "$150 per additional revision round"
4. **Have approval checkpoints:**
   - Approve layout before modeling
   - Approve materials before final renders

**In contract:**
> "Client approves layout. Then client approves materials. Then final renders produced. Changes after approval constitute additional revision rounds."

## Part 6: Legal Basics

### Contracts (Essential!)

**ALWAYS use a contract. Always.**

**Minimum contract elements:**
1. **Parties** (you and client names)
2. **Scope of work** (what you'll do)
3. **Deliverables** (what they get)
4. **Timeline** (when delivered)
5. **Payment** (amount and schedule)
6. **Revisions** (how many included)
7. **Cancellation** (terms for ending)
8. **Ownership** (who owns the files)
9. **Signatures** (both parties)

**Where to get:**
- **Lawyer** (best, $500-1500)
- **LegalZoom** ($100-300)
- **Bonsai** ($19/month, contract templates included)
- **Free templates** (Google, customize carefully!)

**Don't skip this to save money. One bad client = huge loss.**

---

### Payment Terms

**Standard structure:**
- **50% deposit** - to start work
- **50% on completion** - before delivery

**Alternative for large projects:**
- **33% deposit** - to start
- **33% at midpoint** - concept approval
- **34% on completion** - final delivery

**NEVER deliver files before final payment!**

---

### Business Structure

**Start: Sole Proprietor**
- Easiest setup
- Report on personal taxes
- No separation of personal/business assets

**When growing: LLC**
- Liability protection
- Professional image
- Tax benefits
- Cost: $50-500 to form (varies by state)

**Consult accountant when:**
- Making $30K+/year from design
- Have significant expenses
- Want to optimize taxes

---

### Taxes

**You'll owe:**
- **Income tax** (federal, state, local)
- **Self-employment tax** (~15.3%)

**Track everything:**
- Income (every payment)
- Expenses (software, hardware, education, office, travel)

**Use accounting software:**
- QuickBooks Self-Employed ($15/month)
- FreshBooks ($15/month)
- Wave (free)

**Save for taxes:**
- Set aside 25-30% of income
- Pay estimated quarterly taxes
- Hire accountant ($300-500/year)

### Specializing

**Generalist:** Can do anything (but not known for anything)  
**Specialist:** Known for one thing (commands premium)

**Profitable niches:**
- **Kitchen & Bath** (high value, repeatable)
- **Commercial Offices** (corporate budgets)
- **Restaurants** (high design, fast-paced)
- **Real Estate** (virtual staging, marketing)
- **Tiny Homes** (growing market, specific skills)
- **Luxury Residential** (high budgets)

**Becoming a specialist:**
1. Do 5-10 projects in niche
2. Build niche portfolio
3. Study niche deeply
4. Market as specialist
5. Charge specialist rates (20-50% higher)

### Scaling Up

**Level 1: Solo** (You do everything)
- Income: $30-80K/year
- Hours: 20-40/week
- Clients: 20-40/year

**Level 2: Efficient Solo** (Systems, tools, boundaries)
- Income: $80-150K/year
- Hours: 30-40/week
- Clients: 40-60/year

**Level 3: Team** (Hire help)
- Income: $150K-500K+/year
- Hours: 40-60/week (more business, less modeling)
- Clients: Unlimited

**When to hire:**
- Turning down work consistently
- Making $150K+/year
- Want to grow
- Have systems documented

**First hire options:**
- Part-time modeler ($25-40/hour)
- Virtual assistant ($15-25/hour)
- Junior designer ($40-60/hour)
